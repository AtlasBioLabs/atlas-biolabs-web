In peptide sourcing, quality is defined not only by the compound itself but by the documentation and testing processes that support it. For commercial buyers, understanding purity levels and how to interpret Certificates of Analysis (COA) is essential for making informed procurement decisions.

This article breaks down how peptide quality is evaluated and what buyers should look for when reviewing documentation in peptide sourcing and supplier review.

![Certificate of analysis and purity review illustration](/blog/inline/peptide-coa-review.jpg)

---

## What defines peptide quality?

Peptide quality is influenced by several factors:

- synthesis accuracy  
- purity level  
- stability  
- consistency across batches  

While a peptide may meet basic specifications, variations in production or handling can affect overall quality.

---

## Understanding purity levels

Purity refers to the percentage of the desired peptide present in a sample relative to impurities.

Common purity benchmarks include:

- >=95% purity  
- >=98% purity  
- higher levels for specialized applications  

Higher purity typically indicates more refined production and purification processes.

---

## What is a Certificate of Analysis (COA)?

A Certificate of Analysis (COA) is a document that provides detailed information about a specific batch of a product.

It typically includes:

- product identification  
- batch or lot number  
- purity percentage  
- testing method  
- date of analysis  

COAs are used by buyers to verify that a product meets expected specifications.

---

## Interpreting COA data

When reviewing a COA, buyers often focus on:

### Purity profile
Indicates the proportion of the target peptide compared to impurities.

### Testing method
Common methods include High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC), which is used to assess purity.

### Batch identification
Ensures traceability and consistency across orders.

---

## Why batch transparency matters

Batch transparency allows buyers to track:

- consistency across orders  
- variations in purity or composition  
- documentation history  

Suppliers who provide batch-level information help buyers maintain quality control within their own processes.

## Additional quality indicators

Beyond purity and COA, buyers may also consider:

- stability under storage conditions  
- packaging integrity  
- compatibility with intended applications  

These factors contribute to overall product reliability.

---

## Quality challenges in global sourcing

Global peptide sourcing introduces variability in:

- manufacturing processes  
- testing standards  
- documentation formats  

To manage these challenges, buyers often work with suppliers who provide consistent documentation and structured product information.

---

## Best practices for evaluating peptide quality

Buyers can improve quality assessment by:

- requesting COA for each batch  
- comparing purity levels across suppliers  
- verifying testing methods  
- ensuring documentation consistency  

---

Suppliers play a critical role in maintaining quality through:

- structured sourcing processes
